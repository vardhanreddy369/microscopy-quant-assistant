Reading:
  Accuracy on real positives is a near-tie — the distribution (Gaussian,
  Gamma, k-means, Otsu) barely matters when a positive population exists.
  The all-negative image is where they part: every ungated method splits
  one population in half and invents 30-50% positives. Only the gated
  method abstains and reports zero. Removing the gate from the same
  Gaussian mixture reproduces the failure, so the population test — not
  the distribution — is the contribution.
