Two analyses. 'mixture' needs no control and is objective on any image. 'control-anchored'
normalises to this plate's own dose-0 wells — the field-standard method for a screen —
and is the gold-standard analysis when controls exist. Anchoring lifts the dose-response
correlation (0.65 -> 0.80, 0.76 -> 0.84) and pins the negative controls near zero (~1%),
which is exactly what normalising to controls is for. The 99th-percentile cut-off is a
standard conservative choice, not tuned to this data.

BBBC013 gives the dose per well, not per-cell labels, so this validates the population
readout — dose-response and control separation — not a per-cell accuracy (that is the
synthetic validation).
